Archimedes: Curiousity-Driven Bayesian Optimization with Joint Active Causal Learning (In Progress)

Research under development. Results will be shared in January 2018.

In a tweet

By jointly learning how to maximize the objective function (Bayesian Optimization) as well as an underlying Structural Causal Model (active learning) you can speed up Bayesian optimization in terms of number of samples needed to converge on the optimum.

Challenge

Bayesian Optimization is a useful optimization tool, but can be computationally expensive due to O(N^3) updating. We wish to guide active learning optimally. The data generating process of the data contains valuable causal information that we might use, but no BO model explicitly takes this extra information into account.

Solution

By learning a Structural Causal Model at the same as Bayesian Optimization, we can set a tradeoff between sampling for optimization and sampling for causal understanding. I am working on two approaches: formulating a new acquisition function, and using multi-task GPs to share information across both tasks.
