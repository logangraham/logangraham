Inferring Work Task Automatability from AI Expert Evidence

[Paper] [Data]

Proceedings of the AAAI/ACM Conference on Artificial Intelligence, Ethics, and Society (AIES) 2019 and the NeurIPS 2018 AI for Social Good Workshop.

In a tweet

We surveyed 150+ machine learning/robotics experts to collect 4500 datapoints on what work tasks are automatable today. We modeled the relationship between task skills, knowledge, and ability and the task automatability.

Challenge

Despite alarm about automation, we don't have good data on what can be automated in the first place. Most approaches focus on occupations, which mistakes the unit of automation: tasks themselves. Further, collecting data to produce a general and nuanced taxonomy of automatability is very expensive.

Solution

We collected a novel dataset and developed a bespoke machine learning approach to this computatational social science problem. We generate automatability labels by using a Bayesian technique to combine multiple uncertain classifiers' (expert labellers') estimates. Then we use an ordinal-likelihood Gaussian Process to model the relationship between activity features and activity automatability labels. We analyse results across work types, occupation groups, income, education. We discuss where the model most disagrees with ground truth labels, and use model gradients to determine the features that drive automatability the most.

Results

The first high-granularity activity automatability dataset of its kind covering every work activity performed by every occupation in the United States. We present the method and raw data for further research into what can be automated.
