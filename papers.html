#27: AI is Going to Kill Everyone Edition

Apologies for the grave title. Three very intriguing things have happened in the past few weeks around AI safety, and I recommend you give them a look and think for yourself where you stand. In other news: another TensorFlow release, a new Distill publication, a cookbook with potential, and the Chinese government's AI strategy.


Interesting News and Links

  • "Humankind's Last Invention Ever": three initiatives were launched to promote the development of beneficial AI and prevent the development of severely harmful AI:
    • The Future of Life Institute released Slaughterbots, a short film associated with AutonomousWeapons.org, to reveal how plausible a militarised AI future is. Extremely engaging video – if you like Black Mirror, you'll enjoy this.
    • Canadian researchers called for the Canadian government to join an international ban on the weaponization of artificial intelligence. Canadian/Canadian-related? You can sign the letter here.
    • On November 3rd, researchers launched the Montreal Declaration for a Responsible Development of Artificial Intelligence. You can contribute your thoughts through contributing a brief or answering a survey.
  • Getting deep on the phone: Google released TensorFlow Lite, which can compile pre-trained TensorFlow models for Android and iOS, which should speed-up mobile deep learning applications.
  • Picture books for adults: a great new Distill publication explores how NN's "build up their understanding of images."
  • Cooking with ML: if you follow/know of Ferenc Huszar (his blog is recommended), he's just put out a short note describing to various types of learning barriers, which is short and nice as a refresher.
  • Central planning for AI: the Chinese State Council released "A Next Generation Artificial Intelligence Development Plan" back in July, which highlights 8 core technologies it aims to build worth the read – see page 9 here.
