Qualia rankings

February 1, 2025


It feels important to catalogue your experiences that really activate the feeling of being alive, for better or for worse. The experiences in this list all had something distinct about them, a sort of intensity.


Ranked not by pleasantness, but intensity


The sauna-to-frozen-river plunge circuit at Bota Bota in Montreal with one of my best friends.

Rowing a sprint piece perfectly in sync with the 8 other guys at 5:30am in Oxford.

A perfect full-sun hike & swim day during the Covid summer with 20 close friends.

The feeling of death and accomplishment after showing up to my first CrossFit class and realizing it's the first day of the CrossFit Open.

The moment of producing The One Graph that showed Omicron either meant the end of the pandemic or the beginning of system collapse.

Taking a first whole-body full breath during a breathwork session.

Aerobatic turns while paragliding in Turkey.

An impressively full-pressure rain shower a New York hotel after a long run in sub-freezing temperatures.

Thrill after skiing my first black run in Whistler.

Seeing a wedding party emerge from a dust storm at Burning Man.

The chest-throttling sound of a F-22 Raptor above my head on the rooftop of my sister's apartment.

The taste of a $200 bottle of wine in an "inexpensive student wine bar" in Paris selected by a friend from Bordeaux.

Being slightly drunk on the subway after the Paris wine bar.

Eating the best ramen egg I've had.

Reading two or three chapter-concluding sentences in Richard Rhodes' The Making of the Atomic Bomb that have no right to be as beautiful as he wrote them.

Tipsy whispering at Burp Castle with friends.

Walking the streets of New York.

The 10th StarCraft loss in a row, playing with friends, when a sort of learned helplessness kicks in.

Crying at the climax of One Second After at 2 A.M.

Seeing the buildings of Oxford on my first day as a grad student.

A brief moment during an astral projection meditation.
